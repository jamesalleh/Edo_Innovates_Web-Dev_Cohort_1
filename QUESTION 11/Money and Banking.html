Course: Money and Banking


The money and banking course introduces students to the dynamics of a financial system,
including money, financial markets,
as well as financial institutions and intermediaries. Students learn the functions
and roles of financial institutions in the economy, the conduct of monetary policy,
and the functioning of the Federal Reserve.
They also learn about the calculation of interest rates, as well
as their structure and associated risks.
